Digital Video

Digital Video Artworks

Down below are films I created using digital video techniques from the Art 105 class, taught by Professor G. Craig Hobbs, and Art 75 class, taught by Professor David Bayus.

The Package, 2024, Art 105

For my first project of the class, I created an action short film about a down on his luck protagonist who takes a job to intercept a package for his boss. This short film inhibits video editing and filimg concepts that were taught, such as: shot placement, blending modes, video composting, typography, vector & rasterized graphics, color grading, sound design, transitions, and etc.

Spacehound, 2024, Art 105

For my second project of the class, I created an action short film about a dog who lives in space and chases down falling asteroids which lands on a nearby planet. The focus of the project was to include AI generated works to composite into a short film. This short film inhibits video editing and filimg concepts that were taught, such as: greenscreen, particle effects, 3d camera movment, blending modes, video composting, typography, vector & rasterized graphics, color grading, sound design, and etc.

The Screwdriver, 2022, Art 75

For my final project of the class, I created a comedy horror short film about a screwdriver that falls down from space and is picked up by a group of teenagers who are hunted down for that very screwdriver. The short film inhibits concepts that were taught and used throughout the semester, such as: green screening, motion tracking, color grading, sound design, and etc.

10pm, 2022, Art 75

The purpose of this project is to properly display time in a cinematic story. In this short film, I tell the story of a school boy who comes home from school and does homework until 10pm hits when the school boy transcends into a new world where time starts to speed up.

Shrink Film, 2022, Art 75

For the Shrink Film, I decided to create a short story about how I can shrink my dog with a remote controller and take him around. Using a greenscreen and motion tracking on After Effects, I was successfully able to make my dog appear as if he was the size of my hand.
